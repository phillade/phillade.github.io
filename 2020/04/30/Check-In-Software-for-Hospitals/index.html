Check In Software for Hospitals

Introduction

Taiwan’s health care is one of the most cost efficient health care systems in the world. The universal health care system issues a smart card which holds a myriad of information about the card holder.

A professional solution for checking in patients to the clinic already exist, but costs $5000 USD to install, pictured here:
These devices can be found bolted outside each clinical room. The presented solution is vastly cheaper depending on the hardware used to implement the solution.

I worked with the neurology department at Cheng Hsin hospital to create a check in system for patients who are scheduled for special examinations. All it takes is for the patient to insert the national health insurance card into the card reader and the software takes care of the rest. It even has mandarin speech generated from Amazon Polly to tell the patient to remove the card on a successful check in.

This project features:

  • Audio cue following successful reading of the smart card’s data
  • Constant background polling for the presence of a card
  • Display of a censored name, check in time, and last three digits of patient id (for differentiating people with the same name)

Future improvement:

  • Integrating it with a scheduling system for examinations
