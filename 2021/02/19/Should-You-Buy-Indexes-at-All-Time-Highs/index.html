Should You Buy Indexes at All Time Highs?
Created 2021-02-19| Updated 2021-02-19

I’ve always wondered whether buying into the stock market at all time highs was a good idea. Luckily, I have the tools to answer the question. By back testing on data from Polygon.io, I’m able look at whether or not it is more profitable to immediately buy into the stock market or wait for the prices to retrace. This leads me to the first question I want to answer.

Do stock prices always retrace?

If market prices do not retrace, then that means that I will have missed out on the best price I could have bought at. So, I want to know that if a stock price breaks new all time highs, does the stock price always falls back down to retest?

The stock ticker I chose was VTI, Vanguard’s Total Stock Market Index. For most passive investors, this choice makes an excellent choice for its low expense ratio of 0.03%. This is much lower than SPY’s expense ratio of 0.095%.
